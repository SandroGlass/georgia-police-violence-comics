Police ultraviolence peaks in Georgia

Interactive comics

Georgia's EU accession was halted

On November 28, 2024 thousands took to the streets in Georgia following government's decision to halt EU accession.

They were demanding snap elections and return to the path of EU integration.

The Riot police was quick to follow

As protests erupted, the riot police were deployed to maintain order, leading to tense standoffs with demonstrators.

In the next days, tens of protesters were arrested and hospitalised.

Dozens claim they were beaten and tortured

These claims were confirmed by local and international human rights defenders.

Despite many pending investigations none of the alleged abusers were called to account.

The victims say they demand justice.
